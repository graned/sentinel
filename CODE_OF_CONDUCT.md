# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in Sentinel respectful, constructive, and welcoming for everyone.

We want the project community to be collaborative, curious, and kind, even when disagreements happen.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:
- being respectful and considerate
- assuming good intent while still giving clear feedback
- focusing criticism on ideas, code, and behavior, not people
- being open to correction
- helping create a space where contributors of different backgrounds and skill levels can participate

## Unacceptable behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:
- harassment, intimidation, or discrimination
- personal attacks, insults, or trolling
- sustained disruptive behavior
- publishing others' private information without permission
- sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention
- deliberately hostile or exclusionary conduct

## Project standards

Because Sentinel is security-sensitive infrastructure, we also expect:
- responsible disclosure of vulnerabilities
- good-faith discussion of security trade-offs
- no glorification of abuse, fraud, surveillance misuse, or account compromise

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards.
They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, chat spaces, and other official community channels.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact the project maintainers through the contact channel listed in `SECURITY.md` or the repository profile/contact method.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including warnings, temporary bans, or permanent removal from community spaces.
